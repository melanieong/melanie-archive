```text
So.. a month ago I was heavily considering quitting university. I had already planned to get a job, was telling everyone in job interviews that I was taking a "gap year" with the underlying intention to never return. 

Everyone around me was supporting me. But what I realized was that there was no plan going forward. I wasn't good enough at anything, no one would want to hire me. I read this book called So Good They Can't Ignore You and came to the understanding that I have no "career capital" yet. It doesn't matter what you're passionate about. You can't keep jumping from one thing to another without getting extremely good at one thing. It's a waste of time and you're only doing this because you think that you'll eventually find something you're good at. Turns out that you won't like anything that you're not good at. So get good at something first, decide if you want to switch it up, and your career is much better spent that way. 

That made me want to continue through university but not with the same priorities as before. While before, I went through university trying to experiment and find what I like most, my goal now is to learn as much as possible about everything related to business. There should not be a single rock unturned during this process. I just want to understand businesses and achieve those small wins. Getting my first real job for one, would be an amazing milestone. Another one would be making a whole valuation sheet that I fully understand.

Things will be hard on me for the next couple of months, but I really want to try hard. This is what I will dedicate my whole time to. And when I'm not doing this, I will be trying to build my own brand to practice and use what I learn, or practicing jazz piano. Maybe those things can all be combined at one point in the future. I also don't want to fully give up my art, so I hope I can get good at that too later on. Just not right now. Ciao for now. x Melanie
```
